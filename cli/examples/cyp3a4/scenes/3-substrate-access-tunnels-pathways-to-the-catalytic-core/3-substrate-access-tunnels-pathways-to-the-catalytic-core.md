## 3. Substrate Access Tunnels: Pathways to the Catalytic Core

Deep within CYP3A4, multiple tunnels provide essential pathways for substrates to reach the buried active site and for products to exit after metabolism. These tunnels are surrounded by flexible structural elements, particularly the F and G helices, which can shift to allow ligands to enter the tunnels, which can open or close depending on conformational changes in CYP3A4, reflecting its adaptability.
